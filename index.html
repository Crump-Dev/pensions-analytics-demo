●
The problem: When actuaries modify workbooks, rows get inserted, deleted, or reordered. Cell references like F46 shift to F45 — making naive comparison useless. Our semantic engine resolves references to field names, revealing that the formulas are actually identical.

Row-Agnostic Matching
When actuaries insert or delete rows, every cell reference shifts. Our engine matches by field name, not row number — so F46 becoming F45 is correctly identified as the same calculation.
Formula Decomposition
Every formula is broken down to its semantic components. =ROUND(Input!F46,2) becomes =ROUND('Input'!{Pension_at_NRD},2) — making the actual logic transparent regardless of structure.
Genuine Differences Surface
Once structural noise is stripped away, the 7 real value differences and 23 formula changes stand out clearly — no more false positives from row shifts drowning out real issues.
